I never expected the data to behave like that. We ran the assay three times. The first run failed — contamination, probably — so we tossed it. But the second and third? Clean. And weirdly consistent, which made me suspicious at first. Greene's old paper from 1998 had hinted at this, though nobody followed up. Why not? Hard to say. Funding dried up, people moved on, the usual story. Anyway, what matters is that the effect is real, even if small, and it shows up only when temperature is held below twelve degrees.
