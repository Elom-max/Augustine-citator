## Long description (4000 chars max — the main listing page)

```
Augustine Citation Tool is a focused research utility for academics, theologians, philosophy students and patristics scholars who quote Augustine of Hippo in their writing.

Select from 67 of Augustine's works (Confessions, City of God, On the Trinity, On Christian Teaching, the Soliloquies, the Sermons, the Letters, the anti-Pelagian, anti-Donatist and anti-Manichean treatises, exegetical works, philosophical dialogues, and more), enter the book / chapter / paragraph, and the add-in inserts a properly formatted citation as a footnote, endnote, or inline reference — without you ever leaving the document.

CITATION STYLES — six conventions, each formatted to its own rules:
• CChr SL (KU Leuven house style)
• CCSL (Corpus Christianorum, Series Latina)
• CSEL (Corpus Scriptorum Ecclesiasticorum Latinorum)
• BA (Bibliothèque Augustinienne) — author rendered as "Augustin"
• PL (Migne Patrologia Latina)
• WSA (Works of Saint Augustine) — English title in quotes, "in WSA Vol", as per series convention
Plus a "short note" mode that emits the classical "Aug., conf. X, 27, 38" abbreviation.

PASSAGE BANK — search and reuse passages without retyping:
The add-in ships with 22 canonical passages in public-domain Latin (Patrologia Latina) with French and English translations (Péronne, Pusey, Marcus Dods, Philip Schaff — all out of copyright). Select a work, pick a passage from the dropdown, and the form auto-fills the reference and both languages.

You can also build your own private passage bank by pasting from Brepols' Corpus Augustinianum Gissense (CAG) — the add-in's "Import CAG" button parses the pasted reference, detects the work and the lib/cap/par numbers, and saves the passage to your local library. Your custom passages stay on your device; they are never uploaded or shared.

PRACTICAL FEATURES:
• Multilingual UI: French, English, Dutch
• Roman numerals for books (toggleable)
• Composition date in brackets (toggleable)
• City of publication for pre-CMOS 18 style (toggleable)
• Citation history — re-insert any previous citation with one click
• Clipboard fallback if Word is offline
• No account, no sign-in, no telemetry, no data sent to any server other than the static taskpane host

CONVENTIONS:
This tool follows the abbreviation conventions of the KU Leuven Institute of Early Christian and Byzantine Studies and the broader patristics community. All citation output is plain-Office HTML so it interoperates with Zotero, EndNote and Word's native footnote system.

This tool is provided free for academic use.
```
